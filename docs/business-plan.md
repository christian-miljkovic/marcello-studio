# Marcello — Business Plan

*Boutique web studio for fashion brands. Founded by Christian Marcello Miljkovic, New York, 2026.*
*Written 2026-07-13. A living document — revise as real client data comes in.*

## 1. The opportunity

The founders of HAITCH (NYC menswear label, current client) describe the gap directly: it is hard to find developers who can actually build, have an eye, and stay high touch with the client. The market confirms it:

- Fashion-capable agencies (Sweden Unlimited, Vaan Group, Commerce-UI) start at $60k+ and won't take small labels.
- Marketplace freelancers are cheap but produce template-grade work that embarrasses a brand whose whole business is taste.
- The middle — agency-quality output from a single senior operator who answers the phone — is nearly empty.

Prospect research (2026-07-13) found 29 qualified targets in one afternoon: CFDA Fashion Fund finalists with placeholder products live on their stores, NY Men's Day designers with splash-page-only sites, PR firms whose owned domains 301-redirect to Instagram. Demand is not the constraint; capacity is.

## 2. Positioning

**A senior, high-touch digital partner for emerging fashion brands that need taste, technical depth, and one accountable person from concept through launch.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Ideal client** | Founder-led fashion, jewelry, accessories, or lifestyle brand with strong product and imagery, roughly $500K–$15M in annual sales, and a website that no longer matches its ambition |
| **Primary wedge** | Custom Shopify redesigns and brand launches combining editorial storytelling, conversion-minded commerce, and unusually close founder collaboration |
| **Referral wedge** | White-label or referral partnerships with fashion PR firms, showrooms, creative directors, photographers, and brand consultants |
| **Differentiator** | Product strategy + senior engineering + design judgment. No junior handoff, no telephone game, no generic template wearing expensive perfume |
| **Operating model** | Focused boutique studio. Christian leads strategy, UX, and development hands-on; trusted photography, copy, motion, or visual-design specialists brought in only when a project requires them |

- Speak the industry's visual language (the portfolio site itself is the first proof).
- Never publish prices — fashion studios lead with client names, not rate cards.
- Anchor credibility on HAITCH (headless Next.js + Shopify storefront) and Non Grata Magazine (interactive 3D editorial site).

## 4. The offer ladder

Benchmarked 2026-07 against published agency pricing (Ask Phill, Roboto Studio, Weaverse) and NYC senior-freelance rates. Effective internal rate: **$175–$225/hr** — use it to sanity-check any custom scope, never quote it.

| Offer | Best for | Core deliverables | Timeline | Price (start → target) | Payment terms | Strategic purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Digital Direction Sprint** | Brands unsure what to rebuild | Audit, customer journey, visual direction, site map, prioritized roadmap | 1–2 wks | **$2,500 → $3,500** | 100% upfront | Paid discovery that filters serious clients and creates the brief |
| **Launch Site** | New brand or small catalog | Brand site or lightly customized Shopify, 5–8 templates/pages, mobile QA, analytics | 4–6 wks | **$8,000 → $12,000** | 50 / 30 / 20 | Accessible entry product without becoming a bargain basement |
| **Signature Commerce** | Growing fashion brand | Custom Shopify UX, collection/PDP/cart, editorial modules, integrations, launch support | 8–12 wks | **$18,000 → $28,000** | 40 / 30 / 20 / 10 | Core flagship offer |
| **Digital Flagship** | Established brand or ambitious relaunch | Highly custom interactions, headless/custom architecture where justified, internationalization, advanced integrations | 12–20+ wks | **$35,000 → $75,000** | 30 / 30 / 25 / 15 | Selective premium engagements |
| **Care + iteration retainer** | Any client post-launch | Ongoing iteration, fixes, seasonal updates | monthly | **$2k–$4k/mo** (floor $1k–$1.5k maintenance-only) | monthly in advance | The run-rate engine; attach to every build |

Guardrails from the market research: headless architecture only clearly pays off above ~$5M brand revenue — Signature Commerce is the honest default for most of the prospect list; never quote a Flagship under $25k. Sprint fees credit toward a build. Lead-to-offer mapping lives in `lead-criteria.md`.

## 5. Capacity and pipeline math (side business — be honest)

Christian has a full-time job. Realistic capacity: **~10–15 focused hrs/wk → one active build + 1–2 retainers at any time.**

Year-one revenue scenarios:

| Scenario | Mix | Annual |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 3 brand sites ($8k avg) + 1 theme build ($22k) + 2 retainers × 6mo ($2k) | **$70k** |
| Base | 4 brand sites + 1 theme build + 3 retainers × 8mo | **$102k** |
| Stretch (requires cutting scope elsewhere) | 2 theme builds + 3 brand sites + 4 retainers | **$150k+** |

Funnel assumption: 10 personalized emails/wk → ~20% reply → ~2 calls/wk → ~1 paid discovery/mo → ~1 build per quarter + retainer attach. Adjust with real data after the first 30 emails.

## 7. Risks and honest flags

- **Employment agreement.** Review the Slang agreement for moonlighting/IP-assignment clauses *before invoicing anyone*. Keep all Marcello work off employer hardware, accounts, and hours. Studio email only — never christian@slang.ai.
- **Capacity collision.** One bad week at the day job stalls a client build. Mitigate: generous timelines in proposals (2× internal estimate), retainers over big-bang builds.
- **Fashion budgets are real but small.** Many target brands are pre-revenue. The discovery sprint and $6k–$12k tier exist so a "no budget" is still a relationship.
- **Key-person everything.** No delivery redundancy. Do not sign two simultaneous builds.
- **HAITCH permission.** Nothing that names HAITCH ships publicly without their written OK.

## 8. What Marcello is not (YAGNI)

No subcontractors, no productized-subscription tier, no SEO/ads services, no non-fashion clients chased. Revisit only when the base scenario is consistently hit for two consecutive quarters.
